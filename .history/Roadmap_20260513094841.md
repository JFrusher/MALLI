# M.A.L.L.I. Project Roadmap
## Mobile Advanced Lightweight Localization & Imaging
**A Biomedical Engineering Initiative for Accessible Malaria Diagnostics**

---

## 1. Project Vision
To bridge the gap between high-end digital pathology and rural point-of-care settings by combining low-cost physical optics with optimized Edge-AI (MobileNetV3).

---

## 2. Technical Roadmap

### Phase 1: Data Engineering & "Dry Lab" Optimization
**Goal:** Build a robust, noise-resilient classification engine.
* **Dataset Acquisition:** Source the NIH Malaria Dataset (27,558 cell images).
* **Preprocessing for the Field:**
    * Implement **Stain Normalization**: Field stains vary in pH and quality. Use algorithms to normalize color distribution.
    * **Synthetic Noise Injection:** Add Gaussian noise, motion blur, and chromatic aberration to training data to simulate low-cost lenses.
* **Model Architecture:**
    * Implement **MobileNetV3-Small** in PyTorch/TensorFlow.
    * Utilize **Transfer Learning** from ImageNet weights.
* **Optimization:**
    * **Post-Training Quantization (PTQ):** Convert to 8-bit Integer (INT8) for the mobile NPU.
    * Export to `.tflite` or `.onnx` formats.

### Phase 2: Optical Engineering (The Hardware Bridge)
**Goal:** Achieve ~400x total magnification (optical + digital) for < $20.
* **Lens Selection:**
    * *Option A:* **Foldscope** (Paper-based microscopy).
    * *Option B:* **Ball Lens Retrofit** (3mm sapphire or glass ball lens).
* **Lighting & Contrast:**
    * Design a 3D-printable clip to align the phone camera with the lens.
    * Implement a simple LED backlighting rig with a diffuser.

### Phase 3: Mobile Application Development
**Goal:** A seamless, offline-first diagnostic interface.
* **Real-Time Inference:**
    * Use the **TFLite Support Library** for real-time camera stream processing.
* **Feature Set:**
    * **Auto-Capture:** Trigger image capture only when the frame is in focus.
    * **Offline Mode:** Ensure the model is bundled with the app for use in remote areas.
